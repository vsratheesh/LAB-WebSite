Overview

Indian Engineering Services (IES) exam, also known as Engineering Service Examination (ESE), is an all India entrance examination conducted every year by the Union Public Service Commission for the recruitment of Indian Engineering Services. Candidates selected through ESE are posted as Assistant Executive Engineers/Assistant Engineers in various government departments such as Indian Railways, Army, Navy, etc.

IES engineers will get high respect and stature in the society. Candidates who are selected for ESE are appointed in various departments under Central government for Group A and Group B posts.

We will help you to choose a career with their respective eligibility in 10 easy steps. It helps you more in comparing yourself with the eligibility of any career. This document will easily help you to hold knowledge on how to get into IES and its eligibility criteria.

Ten Things

Eligibility Criteria

Academic Qualification:B.Tech in ECE.

Age Limit:21 – 30 years for general category. It is higher for other categories such as SC, ST, OBC and Physically Handicapped.

Work Experience:Not required but helps during posting.

Physical Standards:Candidates have to pass the medical exam. Physical standards are not high. For instance, height requirement is minimum 152 cm for male and 200 cm for female, chest expansion 5 cm, etc.

How to Enter

Entrance Exam: General English, General Awareness, ECE.

Apply Directly: No provision to apply directly to any department. Selection is made by UPSC through entrance exam.

Through A Reference:Reference does not work for entering into IES.

How to Prepare

Written Exam: GS and general English (made easy publications).

ECE Engineering – Made Easy, NPTEL online videos, engineering books.

Personal Interview:Be sure to revise your basics of ECE subjects and be up-to-date with current affairs. Attend as many mock interviews as you can.

Skill Set

You need to be a good with ECE subjects and also possess leadership qualities.

Job Description and Designation

Assistant Executive Engineer, Assistant Works Manager, Assistant Director would be the initial designation. IES is as good as being an administrative officer within some government departments.  However, the range of operations carried out by these Public Enterprises requires technical knowledge application and that is why only engineers are recruited as IES.

Salary& Perks

Salary at entry level is close to Rs.50,000. Engineers in the Indian Railways have facilities that even IAS and IPS officers envy. This includes housing, medical, education facilities for children, etc.

Career Progression

Executive Engineer -- Superintending Engineer -- Deputy General Manager -- Chief Engineer level-2 – Chief Engineer -- Engineer-in-Chief -- Chairman / Director General / Managing Director (equivalent to Secretary to Government of India).

Pros and Cons

Pros Cons
Best perks Posting in remote areas
Recognition in society No performance based promotion
Timely promotion Monotonous (sometimes)
Administrative Role Red tape
Contribution to society Too much of traveling

The Don’ts

  • Don’t take your job for granted. As long as you are in government service, you have to respect your service rules, be transparent and work hard.
  • Don’t assume that IES is cake walk. In some roles, the work is more than that you may have in private sector.

Career Switch

IES offers opportunity to work at different levels in government. If you wish to move to other career, you will have the option of entering the private sector, become a professor or go for higher studies such as Ph.D abroad.

Selection Process

IES exam has two sections

  • Written Exam
  • Written Exam

  • Written exam consist of two stages one is objective type and the other is conventional paper. IES exam will be conducted for 1000 marks for both objective and conventional divisions. Important topics for ECE graduates while preparing for IES exam (for both conventional and objective):

Structure of exam:

IES Exam consist 5 papers (3 objective papers for each paper 2 hours duration+ 2 descriptive papers of 3 hours duration each).

  • First day:General Studies + General English complete objective paper, for 200 marks

  • Second day:

    Paper I for 200 marks and Paper II for 200 marks of Objective type technical.

  • Third day:Paper I for 200 marks and Paper II for 200 marks of Descriptive type.

Paper1

  • Materials and components
  • Physical Electronics, ICs
  • Signals and systems
  • Network Theory
  • Electromagnetic theory

Paper 2

  • Analog electronic Circuits
  • Digital Electronic circuits
  • Control systems
  • Microwave engineering
  • Computer engineering

    • You should prepare general English and general aptitude as well to clear the objective paper.

  • Interview will be for 200 marks. The candidates who clear the written exam are only eligible to attend the interview.

  • We can’t say specific tips for preparation of interview but you must focus on building your personality. Generally, questions will be asked about your self- like which subject do you like much, your Educational background, what are your positives and negatives, how you will solve if some problem arises in certain situation, overall career objective, etc. This kind of questions will be asked in the interview so be prepared for those.
  • In order to get placed in IES jobs you need to get marks more than 1000 out of 1200.
Career Options

Career Opportunities after Electronics & communication Engineering

Those who have completed graduation in Electronics & communication Engineering can seek employment in following departments:

  • Junior Telecom officer in Indian Telecommunication Service
  • Indian Railway Stores Service (Telecommunication/ Electronics Engineering Posts)
  • Central Power Engineering Service
  • Assistant Naval Stores Officer Grade- I
  • Indian Inspection Service Group
  • Indian Telecommunication Service
Job Roles

The initial position for which the candidates who qualified IES are recruited would be the Assistant Director/Assistant Executive Engineer or equivalent. The promotions will be based on many factors including the IES Rank, work performance, confidential reports and seniority. The common designations are:

  • Assistant Executive Engineer
  • Assistant Works Manager
  • Assistant Director

Job Description: 

In PSUs, higher level management roles are generally assumed by senior-level employees and new entrants will mostly have to deal with technical tasks. However, for engineers recruited through IES have both technical and administrative dimensions go hand in hand right from the entry level.

  • The IES officer would have handle situations related co-ordination with various organs of the PE, communication with the government authorities.
  • Good relationship with other similar Public Enterprises or private parties, etc.
  • Decisions related procurement, product specification and verification, expansion and scrutiny of public works sanctioned by the government, etc., lie with the IES.

Thus, the IES is as good as being an administrative officer within a government department.  Of course, the range of operations carried by these Public Enterprises requires technical knowledge application and that is why only engineers are recruited as IES. 

How to Prepare

Preparation steps

  • Analyse the exam pattern that you are going to appear.
  • You need to put a lot of hard work and dedication to crack the IES exam.
  • Solve the previous exam papers to know the difficulty level of the exam.
  • If you are in the final year, go and join in a good coaching institute, it will help to achieve your goal.
  • Don’t forget to go for the test series.
Pros&Cons

Pros

  • Job security and high salary.
  • Job security and high salary. While travelling, IES officers receive first class accommodation in Railway guest houses nearby all stations within the country. They can travel along with family throughout the country.
  • IES officers are provided with gardeners, sweeper, porter, etc.
  • They will have accommodation provided in bungalows that runs acres of land.
  • You will get Status/Respect in the society as you are in a prestigious position.
  • A person gets immense work satisfaction when a service allows him to touch lives of millions of people.

Cons

  • Initially the posting will be in the remote areas
  • For the starting 3 years you will not get a personal vehicle.
  • Most of the staffs including domestic helps are too aged to be asked for overtime. IES officers are learned and mature enough to appreciate this simple fact.

Selection Process?

The selection process of candidates who appear for the Engineering Services Examination is based on two levels.

  • Written Test

  • Personality Test

Stage-1 Engineering Engineering services (Written Test) (Objective type-1) Hours and Time
Paper-1 (Common for all branches) General Studies and Engineering Aptitude Paper 2 hours 200 Marks
Paper-2 Engineering Discipline-specific Paper 3 hours 300 Marks
Stage-1 Sub Total 500 Marks
Only those candidates qualifying at this Stage1 to be permitted to appear for Stage-II examination
Stage-2 ES- Conventional type papers
Paper-I Engineering Discipline-specific paper-I 3 hours 300 Marks
Paper-II Engineering Discipline-specific paper-II 3 hours 300 Marks
Stage-2 Sub Total 600 Marks
Sthage-1+Stage-2 Sub Total 1100 Marks
Only those candidates qualifying at this Stage1&2 to be permitted to appear for Stage-3 examination
Stage-3 Personality Test 200 Marks
Stage-1+Stage-2+Stage-3 Sub Total 1300 Marks

Stage-2:

Paper-I

  • Building Materials

  • Solid Mechanics

  • Structural Analysis

  • Design of Steel Structures

  • Design of Concrete and Masonry structures

  • Construction Practice, Planning and Management

Paper-II

  • Flow of Fluids, Hydraulic Machines and Hydro Power

  • Hydrology and Water Resources Engineering

  • Environmental Engineering

    • Water Supply Engineering

    • Waste Water Engineering

    • Solid Waste Management

    • Air, Noise pollution and Ecology

  • Geo-technical Engineering and Foundation Engineering 

    • Geo-technical Engineering

    • Foundation Engineering

  • Surveying and Geology

    • Surveying

    • Geology

  • Transportation Engineering

    • Other than these subjects you have to concentrate on General Studies, General English and Aptitude to crack IES exam.

STAGE-3

Personality Test: Candidates who qualify the written exam are called for the interview. Interview carries 200 marks. Officially it is called "Personality Test".

Eligibility: Minimum 21 years and maximum 30 years and you have to obtain a degree in engineering from a recognized university. Salary at the initial stage would be around 21,400-36,000/- Rs per month.

  • Central Civil & Civil Engineering Service

  • Indian Defence Service of Engineers

  • Indian Supply Service

Overview

IES Jobs

Indian Engineering Services (IES) exam, also known as Engineering Service Examination (ESE), is an all India entrance examination conducted every year by the Union Public Service Commission for the recruitment of Indian Engineering Services. Candidates selected through ESE are posted as Assistant Executive Engineers/Assistant Engineers in various government departments such as Indian Railways, Army, Navy, etc.

IES engineers will get high respect and stature in the society. Candidates who are selected for ESE are appointed in various departments under Central government for Group A and Group B posts.

If you want to know about IES or ESE this article will help you in holding knowledge about IES.

Ten Things

Eligibility Criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering

Age Limit:21 – 30 years for general category. It is higher for other categories such as SC, ST, OBC and Physically Handicapped.

Work Experience: Not required but helps during posting.

Physical Standards:Candidates have to pass the medical exam. Physical standards are not high. For instance, height requirement is minimum 152 cm for male and 150 cm for female, chest expansion 5 cm, etc

How to Enter

Entrance Exam: General English, General Awareness, Mechanical Engineering.

Apply Directly: No provision to apply directly to any department. Selection is made by UPSC through entrance exam.

Through A Reference: Reference does not work for entering into IES.

How to Prepare

Written Exam: GS and General English (made Easy publications)

Mechanical Engineering Made Easy, NPTEL online videos, engineering books.

Personal Interview:Be sure to revise your basics of Mech and be up-to-date with current affairs. Attend as many mock interviews as you can.

Skill Set

You need to be a good with Mech and also possess leadership qualities.

Job Description and Designation

Assistant Executive Engineer, Assistant Works Manager, Assistant Director would be the initial designation. IES is as good as being an administrative officer within some government departments.  However, the range of operations carried out by these Public Enterprises requires technical knowledge application and that is why only engineers are recruited as IES.

Salary& Perks

Salary at entry level is close to Rs.50,000. Engineers in the Indian Railways have facilities that even IAS and IPS officers envy. This includes housing, medical, education facilities for children, etc

Career Progression

Executive Engineer -- Superintending Engineer -- Deputy General Manager -- Chief Engineer level-2 – Chief Engineer -- Engineer-in-Chief -- Chairman / Director General / Managing Director (equivalent to Secretary to Government of India).

The Don’ts

  • Don’t take your job for granted. As long as you are in government service, you have to respect your service rules, be transparent and work hard.
  • Don’t assume that IES is cake walk. In some roles, the work is more than that you may have in private sector

Career Switch

  • IES offers opportunity to work at different levels in government. If you wish to move to other career, you will have the option of entering the private sector, become a professor or go for higher studies such as Ph.D abroad.
Selection Process

The selection process of candidates who appear for the Engineering Services Examination is based on two levels.

Written Test:

It contains 5 papers (3 objective papers of 2 hours duration each + 2 descriptive papers of 3 hours duration each), out of which one objective paper with General Studies, 2 more objective papers with Engineering and 2 descriptive papers with Engineering.

Important subjects a mechanical graduateshould study for IES exam are:

Paper I

  • Thermodynamics
  • Refrigeration and Air-conditioning
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Fluid Machinery and Steam Generators

Paper II

  • Theory of Machines
  • Machine Design
  • Strength of Materials
  • Engineering Materials
  • Production Engineering
  • Industrial Engineering
  • Elements of Computation

    • Other than these subjects you have to concentrate on General Studies, General English and Aptitude to crack IES exam.

Personality Test:

Candidates who qualify the written exam are called for the interview. Interview carries 200 marks. Officially it is called "Personality Test".

Eligibility: Minimum 21 years and maximum 30 years and you have to obtain a degree in engineering from a recognized university. Salary at the initial stage would be around 21,400-36,000/- Rs per month.

Career Options

Candidates who have taken Mechanical engineering as a specialization can get job in the Group A (Mechanical Engineering posts) in the following departments:

  • Central Engineering Service (Roads)
  • Indian Railway Service of Mechanical Engineers
  • Indian Naval Armament Service
  • Central Power Engineering Service
  • Electrical and Mechanical Engineers, a branch in the Indian Army
  • Central Electrical & Mechanical Engineering Service
  • Geological Survey of India
  • Indian Navy
  • Indian Defence Service of Engineers
  • Indian Supply Service
Job Roles

The initial position for which the candidates who qualified IES are recruited would be the Assistant Director/Assistant Executive Engineer or equivalent. The promotions will be based on many factors including the IES Rank, work performance, confidential reports and seniority. The common designations are:

  • Assistant Executive Engineer
  • Assistant Works Manager
  • Assistant Director

Job Description: 

In PSUs, higher level management roles are generally assumed by senior-level employees and new entrants will mostly have to deal with technical tasks. However, for engineers recruited through IES have both technical and administrative dimensions go hand in hand right from the entry level.

  • The IES officer would have handle situations related co-ordination with various organs of the PE, communication with the government authorities.
  • Good relationship with other similar Public Enterprises or private parties, etc.
  • Decisions related procurement, product specification and verification, expansion and scrutiny of public works sanctioned by the government, etc., lie with the IES.

Thus, the IES is as good as being an administrative officer within a government department.  Of course, the range of operations carried by these Public Enterprises requires technical knowledge application and that is why only engineers are recruited as IES.
