Overview

Core - IT

An Electrical engineer is not restricted to automobile/power or allied sectors. As said earlier, there are many fields where an Electrical engineer’s knowledge is applicable. In design, production, manufacturing, supply-chain management and maintenance of machines etc. To understand and implement such projects, one is expected to have knowledge in both Electrical engineering and IT.

Many people after pursuing their B.Tech are going either for higher studies or for IT jobs but core jobs are neglected because lack of information. When we asked people about core jobs their answers were like:

  • Very few options available
  • We are trying but no response from the companies even after cracking the interview.
  • No large openings for EEE in core companies
  • They will not offer high salaries like IT
  • Huge competition of a single post

Here we give you the different core jobs that are available, how to get through them, eligibility, career growth, pay pack, etc,.

Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification:B.Tech in Electrical Engineering

Age Limit:No specific limit mentioned but generally between 21 and 25 years

Work Experience:Generally freshers are taken for the roles like Assistant engineer, system design engineer etc. For roles such as ERP consultant (Eg: Oracle Apps), prior work experience of at least 2 years is required in Electrical domain.

Physical Standards:These careers are less physically demanding than core jobs in automobile or thermal sectors in which you may be expected to exercise physically.

How to enter?

Entrance Exam:Quantitative aptitude, reasoning, general knowledge, domain knowledge (in Electrical engineering) are tested in recruitment test.

Apply Directly: Generally, one needs to pass in the recruitment test even when one applies directly to the company.

Through A Reference: In small companies, you can get a job through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through recruitment test.

How to prepare?

Written Exam: Aptitude and Reasoning refer R S Agarwal Electrical/IT subjects – AutoCAD, MATLAB as they are used for designing

Personal Interview: Revise your core subjects thoroughly and gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

You need to be good with at least a few software tools (such as AutoCAD, Kiel etc.) in addition to knowing your core subjects. You are also expected to know the basics of computers thoroughly.

Job Description & Designation

You should work on tools for designing, production, operations, etc. Designation at entry level includes Systems Engineer, Assistant Systems Engineer, Graduate Engineer, Associate Engineer, etc

Salary and Perks

Salary ranges from 1.5 lakh to 5 lakh depending on the company. One of the most important perks of working in IT-Electrical jobs is that you get to stay in cities. Some companies even provide transport, house, etc.

Career Progression

Entry-level Second-level
Electrical engineer Senior Electrical engineer
Operations Manager
Senior Electrical Design engineer
Electrical Design engineer
ERP Consultant

Pros&Cons

Pros Cons
Better pay More stress
Onsite opportunities Highly competitive
Better working conditions Monotonous (sometimes)
Good learning Not core Electrical job
Use Electrical knowledge
Faster growth

The Don'ts

Don’t Neglect your core branch. In case you wish to switch career in future, it might come to your rescue.

Don’t get stuck with one company. Remember that in IT, you’ll have more opportunities than you do in Electrical. Salary also increases as you move from one company to another.

Career Switch

You have the option of the going into core, IES, PSU/PSEs, other careers such as Civil Services, Armed Forces, etc.

Remember that scope in IT-Electrical careers is less in government sectors when compared to private sector.

Roles of Design Engg

Roles of Electrical design engineers:

  • Design, implement, maintain, and improve electrical instruments, equipment, facilities, components, products, and systems for commercial, industrial, and domestic purposes.
  • Operate computer-assisted engineering and design software and equipment to perform engineering tasks.
  • Coordinate with manufacturing, construction, installation, maintenance, support, documentation, and testing activities to ensure compliance with specifications, codes, and customer requirements.
  • Inspect completed installations and observe operations, to ensure conformance to design and equipment specifications and compliance with operational and safety standards.
  • Prepare and study technical drawings, specifications of electrical systems, and topographical maps to ensure that installation and operations conform to
  • Conduct field surveys and study maps, graphs, diagrams, and other data to identify and correct power system problems.

Some of the roles of a Civil engineer are

Generally Civil engineer can work in
  • Designing
Designing

Civil engineers use drafting (also known as technical drawing) in designing of products and creating instruction for the parts of machines. A technical drawing essentially includes all the dimensions to manufacture a part. It can either be hand-drawn or computer-modelled.

A technical drawing can be a computer model or hand-drawn schematic showing all the dimensions necessary to manufacture a part, as well as assembly notes, a list of required materials, and other pertinent information.

2D Modeling

AutoCAD software:

It helps in 2D drafting and 2D drawing helps one to create and edit technical drawings, as well as annotate designs. These tools are used by drafters in activities such as development of floor plans, building permit drawings, building inspection plans, and landscaping layouts.

  • Draft designs more quickly, without using stencils and technical drawing instruments
  • Develop site plans, refine concepts, sketch technical drawings, and share ideas
  • Customize templates and drawings more precisely

Tool used

  • 2D Modeling:
    • AutoCAD

3D Modeling:

It is the 3D computer graphics,3D modeling (or modeling) is the process of developing a mathematical representation of any three-dimensional  surface of an object (either inanimate or living) via specialized software The product is called a 3D model. It can be displayed as a two-dimensional image through a process called 3D rendering  or used in a computer simulation  of physical phenomena. The model can also be physically created using 3D printing  devices.

Models may be created automatically or manually. The manual modeling process of preparing geometric data for 3D computer graphics is similar to plastic arts  such as sculpting.

Core - IT Overview

A Mechanical Engineer is not restricted to automobile/power or allied sectors. As said earlier, there are many fields where a mechanical engineer’s knowledge is applicable. In design, production, manufacturing, supply-chain management and maintenance of machines etc. To understand and implement such projects, one is expected to have knowledge in both mechanical engineering and IT.

Many people after pursuing their B.Tech are going for higher studies or for IT jobs but they are not applying for core jobs because of lack of information.

  • We are trying but no response from the companies even after cracking the interview
  • No large openings for Mech in core companies
  • They will not offer high salaries like IT
  • Huge competition of a single post
Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering.

Age Limit: Varies from company to company but for entry-level it is generally between 21 and 25 years.

Work Experience:Either a fresher or one who is experienced can enter the core field. Companies selecting students through on campus interview take freshers while some companies take only experienced candidates.

Physical Standards: Core jobs are more physically demanding since you may be required to stand for long hours and perform physically strenuous tasks while working on machines.

How to enter?

Entrance Exam: Companies primarily test knowledge in mechanical engineering. Students should be thorough with subjects such as automobile engineering, thermal engineering and tools such as AutoCAD, SolidWorks, etc.

Apply Directly:Students can apply to companies such as Tata Motors, Maruti Suzuki, Hyundai, Honda, etc., directly after completing B.Tech. The percentage requirement for core sector companies and core roles is generally higher than for non-core roles.

Through A Reference:In small companies, you can get in through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through recruitment test.

How to prepare?

Written Exam: Depending on the nature of the job and company profile, students may be tested in various subjects such as refrigeration & air conditioning, automotive, Electrical-Mechanical careers etc. For Aptitude and Reasoning refer R S Agarwal.

Personal Interview:Revise your core subjects thoroughly, especially those mentioned in the above ‘Entrance Exam’ section and try to gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

Core companies expect students to be sound with basic concepts of mechanical engineering. Depending on the profile of the company and the role for which the student is being taken, companies may test his/her knowledge on a particular topic.

Salary and Perks

Top core companies such as Honda, Tata Motors pay decent packages while small companies pay much less. Some core sector roles such as quality engineer have the scope of traveling. In addition to bearing the expenses, companies also provide incentives to engineers.

Career Progression

Entry-level Secondary-level
Junior Engineer Senior Engineer
Engineer
Assistant Engineer
Supervisor

Pros Cons

With the experience of working in the core sector you can get into IES, state or Central PSU/PSEs very easily.
