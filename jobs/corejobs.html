Overview

Electronics and communication engineering graduates who have passion for their core subjects often wish to enter careers in the private sector like Communication, power, Designing or other allied sectors.

We will help you to choose a career with their respective eligibility in 10 easy steps. It helps you more in comparing yourself with the eligibility of any career. This document will easily help you to hold knowledge on how a core sector works and its eligibility criteria.

There are a number of sub areas present under ECE field where one, may get job opportunity in the following fields

  • Microelectronics
  • Photonics
  • Embedded
  • VLSI
  • Design
  • Microwave
  • Communication and networking
Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in Electronics Engineering

Age Limit: No specific limit mentioned but generally between 21 and 25 years

Work Experience: Generally freshers are taken in the roles like software engineer, system engineer. For roles such as ERP consultant (Eg: Oracle Apps), prior work experience of at least 2 years is required in ECE domain.

Physical Standards:These careers are less physically demanding than core jobs in automobile or thermal sectors in which you may be expected to exert physically.

How to Enter

Entrance Exam:Quantitative aptitude, reasoning, general knowledge, domain knowledge (in Electronics engineering) are tested in recruitment test.

Apply Directly:Generally, one needs to pass in the recruitment test even when one applies directly to the company.

Through A Reference: In small companies, you can get a job through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through recruitment test.

How to Prepare

Written Exam: Aptitude and Reasoning – R S Agarwal, ECE subjects – DSP, DIP, Embedded C as they are used for designing

Personal Interview:Revise your core subjects thoroughly and gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

You need to be good with at least a few software courses related to your technical subjects. You are also expected to know the basics of C, C++, JAVA and few tools like MATLAB, XILINX thoroughly.

Job Description&Designation

You should work on tools for designing, production, operations, etc. Designation at entry level includes Systems Engineer, Assistant Systems Engineer, Graduate Engineer, Associate Engineer, etc.

Salary and Perks

Salary ranges from 1.5 lakh to 5 lakh depending on the company. One of the most important perks of working in ECE jobs is that you get to stay in cities. Some companies even provide transport, house, etc.

Here is the list of different careers

Digital Signal processing tools:

Digital Signal Processing (DSP) is used in a wide variety of applications like Telephony, Radar, Audio, Digital TC, Multimedia, Fax, Process control etc.

In DSP related jobs a candidate has to deal with signal frequencies and audio range of those signals. The main advantages of DSP are common to many digital systems and include

  • Versatility
  • Repeatability

Digital Image Processing (DIP)

In this, the role will be converting the image in to digital form and performing some operations on it, in order to get an enhanced image or to extract some information from it.

Purpose of Image processing

The purpose of image processing is divided into 5 groups. They are:

  • Visualization - Observe the objects that are not visible.
  • Image sharpening and restoration - To create a better image.
  • Image retrieval - Seek for the image of interest.
  • Measurement of pattern – Measures various objects in an image.
  • Image Recognition – Distinguish the objects in an image.

  • Digital cameras

  • Very large scale integrated circuit (VLSI)

    • Very large-scale integration (VLSI) is the process of integrating or embedding hundreds of thousands of transistors on a single silicon semiconductor microchip.

Career in VLSI

Electronics engineers who are strong in electronics design fundamentals, and have mathematical and analytical aptitude, coupled with an interest in design and verification, can grow into a good VLSI talent.

A VLSI engineer is expected to know the physics of semiconductor devices, linear systems, probability and random variables, engineering mathematics (Fourier, Laplace and Z transforms), circuit analysis and engineering electromagnetic.

Different Designation in VLSI:

  • VLSI Engineer
  • Electronic Engineer
  • Embedded VLSI engineer
  • VLSI CAD Engineer
  • Design Engineer

Work in VLSI Industry

VLSI front end jobs:

  • You may be working as a layout engineer, where you will be designing the digital and analog circuits inside the chip, say oscillator units for a microcontroller, or timer modules for a microcontroller.

VLSI back end roles:

  • You may also work on testing the designs for speed, noise etc and be called a test engineer.
  • Alternatively you can be a RF engineer and be designing Wireless modules, antennas, RF amplifiers

Major Recruiters:

The major recruiters in these fields are Motorola, Philips Semiconductor, Qualcomm, Wipro, TCS, Cadence and Cisco etc.

Electronic design automation (EDA) 

Development:

They work on developing EDA tools which will be used for designing chips, companies like Cadence, Mentor Graphics and Synopsys are major players in this domains, People who has strong skills such as C, C++, data structure and algorithms usually enters into development.

Within EDA engineers works on different tool development which are

  • Verification tools
  • Infrastructure related automation tool
  • Placement engine
  • STA (Static Timing Analysis)
  • Extraction

Core careers for Civil Engineers

Civil engineering is not just about buildings and bridges, it is more about serving your nation to give better infrastructure and designing innovations. Civil engineering graduates who have passion for their core subjects often wish to enter careers in the private sector like automobile, thermal power, mining or other allied sectors.

We will help you to choose a career with their respective eligibility in 10 easy steps. It helps you more in comparing yourself with the eligibility of any career. This document will easily help you to hold knowledge on how a core sector works and its eligibility criteria.

Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in Civil Engineering

Age Limit: No specific limit mentioned but generally between 21 and 25 years.

Work Experience: Either a fresher or one who is experienced can enter the core field. Companies selecting students through on campus interview take fresher’s while some companies take only experienced candidates.

Physical Standards: Core jobs are more physically demanding since you may be required to stand for long hours and perform physically strenuous tasks while working on machines.

How to enter?

Entrance Exam: Companies primarily test knowledge in Civil engineering. Students should be thorough with subjects such as Strength of materials, Fluid Mechanics, Surveying, Engineering materials, Structural analysis, Foundation engineering, Hydraulics engineering etc.

Apply Directly: Students can apply to companies such as My home constructions, Nagarjuna constructions, L&T, ILFS etc., directly after completing B.Tech. The percentage requirement for core sector companies and core roles is generally higher than for non-core roles.

Through A Reference: In small companies, you can get in through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through entrance test.

How to prepare?

Written Exam: Civil Engineering subjects, Aptitude and Reasoning – R S Agarwal.

Personal Interview: Revise your core subjects thoroughly, especially those mentioned in the above ‘Entrance Exam’ section and try to gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

Core companies expect students to be sound with basic concepts of Civil engineering. Depending on the profile of the company and the role for which the student is being taken, companies may test his/her knowledge on a particular topic.

Job Description & Designation

You should work from processing level based on company’s respective product, or If working for any automobile company one should work in car service stations to gain complete knowledge of the product Designation at entry level includes Junior Engineer, Engineer, Assistant Engineer, Supervisor..etc.

Salary and Perks

Top core companies such as Honda, Tata Motors pay decent packages while small companies pay much less. Some core sector roles such as quality engineer have the scope of travelling. In addition to bearing the expenses, companies also provide incentives to engineers.

Power Sector
  • Generation
  • Transmission
  • Distribution

Generation:

The companies like APGenGenco or TS Genco are working for generating electricity using different sources like water, coal, Solar panels etc. Mainly turbines, Generators, Transformers, Circuit breakers etc will be used in the generation part.

Transmission:

The companies like AP Transco or TS Transco will take care of transmitting the generated power in an appropriate level. There will be loss of power if we transmit the generated power as it is. So there are some techniques to avoid these losses:

Generated power can’t be transmitted as it is. We have to adjust the voltage levels like increasing or decreasing the voltage by using step up and step down methods. By varying the voltage current also varies.

The advantage of step up and step down methods is transmitting the voltage with high quality to a maximum number.

Transmitted current should be distributed properly. We use conductors to transmit the current.

Cables and Insulators are mainly used in this transmission process.

DISCOM: Distribution Companies

  • These DISCOMs role companies will take care of transmitting the current from substations to different areas with equal voltage.
  • Transformers, Underground cables, over head cables, Transmission lines are mainly used in these companies.

Companies that comes under DISCOMs:

  • CPDCL - Central power distribution companies Limited
  • NPDCL – Northern power distribution companies limited
  • SPDCL- SOUTHERN POWER DISTRIBUTION COMPANY LIMITED.

Overview

Core Jobs Overview

Mechanical Engineering graduates who have passion for their core subjects often wish to enter careers in the private sector like automobile, thermal power, mining or other allied sectors.

We will help you to choose a career with their respective eligibility in 10 easy steps. These will help you more in comparing yourself with the eligibility of any career. This document is very much helpful for you to know about core sector jobs.

  • Ten things to know
  • Important subjects
  • Salary in core
  • Companies list
  • Pro’s and con’s
Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering.

Age Limit: Varies from company to company but for entry-level it is generally between 21 and 25 years.

Work Experience:Either a fresher or one who is experienced can enter the core field. Companies selecting students through on campus interview take freshers while some companies take only experienced candidates.

Physical Standards: Core jobs are more physically demanding since you may be required to stand for long hours and perform physically strenuous tasks while working on machines.

How to enter?

Entrance Exam: Companies primarily test knowledge in mechanical engineering. Students should be thorough with subjects such as automobile engineering, thermal engineering and tools such as AutoCAD, SolidWorks, etc.

Apply Directly:Students can apply to companies such as Tata Motors, Maruti Suzuki, Hyundai, Honda, etc., directly after completing B.Tech. The percentage requirement for core sector companies and core roles is generally higher than for non-core roles.

Through A Reference:In small companies, you can get in through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through recruitment test.

How to prepare?

Written Exam: Depending on the nature of the job and company profile, students may be tested in various subjects such as refrigeration & air conditioning, automotive, Electrical-Mechanical careers etc. For Aptitude and Reasoning refer R S Agarwal.

Personal Interview:Revise your core subjects thoroughly, especially those mentioned in the above ‘Entrance Exam’ section and try to gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

Core companies expect students to be sound with basic concepts of mechanical engineering. Depending on the profile of the company and the role for which the student is being taken, companies may test his/her knowledge on a particular topic.

Salary and Perks

Top core companies such as Honda, Tata Motors pay decent packages while small companies pay much less. Some core sector roles such as quality engineer have the scope of traveling. In addition to bearing the expenses, companies also provide incentives to engineers.

Pros&Cons

Pros

Direct application of learning from the 4 years of technological study

What one had studied in the academics has direct application in the industry. Though, it is not that one will directly apply the learning but the basics are covered in the institute. So, joining a core sector will be in continuation with what have studied here. Do not presume that your 4 years in engineering will not be of any help later.

Opportunity to work with diverse skill set people

As an engineer in core sector, one has to work with unskilled, semi-skilled and skilled workers as well as with senior management. At times, presentation to VPs and just another time one need to give instructions to the workers with different skill set. Moreover, one also works with people of different nationalities on various occasions.

Future of core sectors

There are various initiatives and policies of government which would lead to the growth of manufacturing sector in the coming years. The National Manufacturing Policy to raise the share of a manufacturing sector in national GDP from 16% to 25 % and to create 100 million additional jobs by 2022. The initiatives such as “Make in India” will attract more investment to make India a Global Manufacturing Hub. India’s National Steel Policy to reach the production level of Steel from present 80 MTPA to 300 MTPA by 2025 this will create more opportunities for a mechanical graduate. 

This clearly states that Future of a Mechanical graduate is filled with many more different sectors with good employment opportunity all over the India

Related to greater welfare

As core sector creates much more jobs. It brings the livelihood to greater proportion of society and has a greater impact on the society in India.

Since ECE is a core branch which have more future options with them after doing their Engineering. They can also go for ESE (Engineering Services Examination).

Cons

Core sector is not lucrative

One of the major factors is the remuneration in any industry. It is true that the initial compensation during the start of the career is not up to the par in core when compared to non-core.

After joining any core industry, one has to go through the training of minimum 6 months. To understand the whole process of the core industry it takes around 2-3 years. This helps one to grow in the career with knowledge, designation and remuneration.

Generally, core sectors are more focused about the commitment on long term orientations example like PF, Perks, Travel allowance, Accommodation, Medical security for self and also for the family. Suppose if one would add their cost along with the salary and all the perks which is the overall remuneration that will be more than most of the non-core or private sector jobs.

Core requires only technical knowledge

There is a belief that core job requires only technical skills, which is actually not true. Soft skills also play a major role in core same like non-core

For example, one should have good communication to present ideas and also listen to other views or one has to be team player to be able to delegate the jobs and take on responsibility. One should also posses ability to take a logical and analytic approach to solving problems and resolving issues.

Core only a blue collar job

Core sector jobs indeed require going on shop floor or site but that is not the only job. It is just a small part of a job. One has to do planning and scheduling various plans, Operating procedures in designing for creating safe environment, making a good annual business plan to present to the management board.  

Poor growth in core sector

There is a belief that core jobs are dead end jobs and there is no way to become successful. There are many persons who started their career in the core field and did quite well. 

Saturation in innovation in core

It is commonly believed that there is saturation in the innovation in core sector. But it is a wrong assumption. Innovation is a continuous process. Innovation doesn't limit just to develop new products. It also includes solving industrial problem, easing the operating procedure, eliminating the manual work to automated system.
