Core Jobs Overview

Mechanical Engineering graduates who have passion for their core subjects often wish to enter careers in the private sector like automobile, thermal power, mining or other allied sectors.

We will help you to choose a career with their respective eligibility in 10 easy steps. These will help you more in comparing yourself with the eligibility of any career. This document is very much helpful for you to know about core sector jobs.

Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering.

Age Limit: Varies from company to company but for entry-level it is generally between 21 and 25 years.

Work Experience:Either a fresher or one who is experienced can enter the core field. Companies selecting students through on campus interview take freshers while some companies take only experienced candidates.

Physical Standards: Core jobs are more physically demanding since you may be required to stand for long hours and perform physically strenuous tasks while working on machines.

How to enter?

Entrance Exam: Companies primarily test knowledge in mechanical engineering. Students should be thorough with subjects such as automobile engineering, thermal engineering and tools such as AutoCAD, SolidWorks, etc.

Apply Directly:Students can apply to companies such as Tata Motors, Maruti Suzuki, Hyundai, Honda, etc., directly after completing B.Tech. The percentage requirement for core sector companies and core roles is generally higher than for non-core roles.

Through A Reference:In small companies, you can get in through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through recruitment test.

How to prepare?

Written Exam: Depending on the nature of the job and company profile, students may be tested in various subjects such as refrigeration & air conditioning, automotive, Electrical-Mechanical careers etc. For Aptitude and Reasoning refer R S Agarwal.

Personal Interview:Revise your core subjects thoroughly, especially those mentioned in the above ‘Entrance Exam’ section and try to gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

Core companies expect students to be sound with basic concepts of mechanical engineering. Depending on the profile of the company and the role for which the student is being taken, companies may test his/her knowledge on a particular topic.

Salary and Perks

Top core companies such as Honda, Tata Motors pay decent packages while small companies pay much less. Some core sector roles such as quality engineer have the scope of traveling. In addition to bearing the expenses, companies also provide incentives to engineers.

Pros&Cons

Pros

Direct application of learning from the 4 years of technological study

What one had studied in the academics has direct application in the industry. Though, it is not that one will directly apply the learning but the basics are covered in the institute. So, joining a core sector will be in continuation with what have studied here. Do not presume that your 4 years in engineering will not be of any help later.

Opportunity to work with diverse skill set people

As an engineer in core sector, one has to work with unskilled, semi-skilled and skilled workers as well as with senior management. At times, presentation to VPs and just another time one need to give instructions to the workers with different skill set. Moreover, one also works with people of different nationalities on various occasions.

Future of core sectors

There are various initiatives and policies of government which would lead to the growth of manufacturing sector in the coming years. The National Manufacturing Policy to raise the share of a manufacturing sector in national GDP from 16% to 25 % and to create 100 million additional jobs by 2022. The initiatives such as “Make in India” will attract more investment to make India a Global Manufacturing Hub. India’s National Steel Policy to reach the production level of Steel from present 80 MTPA to 300 MTPA by 2025 this will create more opportunities for a mechanical graduate. 

This clearly states that Future of a Mechanical graduate is filled with many more different sectors with good employment opportunity all over the India

Related to greater welfare

As core sector creates much more jobs. It brings the livelihood to greater proportion of society and has a greater impact on the society in India.

Since ECE is a core branch which have more future options with them after doing their Engineering. They can also go for ESE (Engineering Services Examination).

Cons

Core sector is not lucrative

One of the major factors is the remuneration in any industry. It is true that the initial compensation during the start of the career is not up to the par in core when compared to non-core.

After joining any core industry, one has to go through the training of minimum 6 months. To understand the whole process of the core industry it takes around 2-3 years. This helps one to grow in the career with knowledge, designation and remuneration.

Generally, core sectors are more focused about the commitment on long term orientations example like PF, Perks, Travel allowance, Accommodation, Medical security for self and also for the family. Suppose if one would add their cost along with the salary and all the perks which is the overall remuneration that will be more than most of the non-core or private sector jobs.

Core requires only technical knowledge

There is a belief that core job requires only technical skills, which is actually not true. Soft skills also play a major role in core same like non-core

For example, one should have good communication to present ideas and also listen to other views or one has to be team player to be able to delegate the jobs and take on responsibility. One should also posses ability to take a logical and analytic approach to solving problems and resolving issues.

Core only a blue collar job

Core sector jobs indeed require going on shop floor or site but that is not the only job. It is just a small part of a job. One has to do planning and scheduling various plans, Operating procedures in designing for creating safe environment, making a good annual business plan to present to the management board.  

Poor growth in core sector

There is a belief that core jobs are dead end jobs and there is no way to become successful. There are many persons who started their career in the core field and did quite well. 

Saturation in innovation in core

It is commonly believed that there is saturation in the innovation in core sector. But it is a wrong assumption. Innovation is a continuous process. Innovation doesn't limit just to develop new products. It also includes solving industrial problem, easing the operating procedure, eliminating the manual work to automated system.
