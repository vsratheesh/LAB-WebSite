Overview

Government Jobs

People are very much interested in government jobs, especially middle class families. Government jobs are preferred because of its Job Security and Pay structure. ECE students can get a job in any central and state government undertakings.

We will help you to choose a career with their respective eligibility in 10 easy steps. It helps you more in comparing yourself with the eligibility of any career. This document will easily help you to hold a knowledge on how to enter into a government sector, its eligibility criteria, career growth, pay pack etc,.

Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification:B.Tech in Electronic Engineering

Age Limit: Varies from career to career.

Work Experience: Both experienced and freshers are taken in government jobs. Job notifications clearly mention the number of years of experience required to apply for the job.

Physical Standards: Physical and medical standards are mentioned in the notification as per the recruitment.

How to Enter

Entrance Exam:General English, General Awareness, Current Affairs and Electronic Engineering

Apply Directly: In most cases, you can apply to the department or organization which advertises vacancies. In some others, selection is done through a separate body.

Through A Reference: Depends on company. Some companies accept applications through reference.

How to Prepare

Written Exam:

  • General Studies/GK – Manorama Year Book,
  • General English – R S Sharma.
  • Electronic Engineering – B.Tech books.

    • Personal Interview:Most jobs have a personal interview. Armed Forces select through a 5-day Services Selection Board interview.

Skill Set

Careers in government sector, especially Armed Forces require one to possess leadership qualities in addition to technical knowledge.

Job Description and Designation

Entry level designations include Assistant Engineer, Executive Engineer, etc. Fresh recruits are expected to perform technical tasks such as production, design, etc. of machines.

Salary and Perks

Entry-level salaries vary from Rs.15, 000 to Rs. 80,000 per month. Most luring perk of any government job is the power and prestige that comes along with it. Many government jobs offer perks such as vehicle, house, etc.

Career Progression

Progression in career depends on department, entry-level designation, number of years of service, etc.

Pros and Cons

Pros Cons
Attractive Perks Posting in remote areas
Pension Not challenging
Medical & education facilities for family Corruption

The Don’ts

Don’t lose patience. In government jobs, career growth may not be as fast as in private companies.

Don’t despair with posting in remote areas. Though in initial years, you’ll be posted in remote areas, as you grow in career, you’ll be posted in metro areas.

Career Switch

You can enter IES, private sector companies or with the experience of working in public sector, you can choose other careers such as Electronic Services, etc.

BSNL

For Direct Recruitment of Junior Telecom Officers, an objective type Examination of 3 hours duration consisting of following sectional papers will be conducted:

SCHEME

A. Engineering Stream Section - I

B. Engineering Stream Section - II

C. General Ability Test Section – III


The syllabus for engineering stream papers will be as given below:

SECTION - I

  • Materials and components:
  • Physical Electronics, Electron Devices and ICs
  • Network theory
  • Electromagnetic Theory
  • Electronic Measurements and instrumentation
  • Power Electronics

SECTION-II

  • Analog Electronic Circuits
  • Digital Electronic Circuits
  • Control Systems
  • Systems
  • Microwave Engineering
  • Computer Engineering
  • Microprocessors

SECTION-III

General ability test

  • The candidate’s comprehension and understanding of General English shall be tested through simple exercises.
  • Questions based on Knowledge about current events.
  • Questions will also be included on events and developments in Telecommunications, History of India and Geography.
  • These will be of a nature, which can be answered without special study by an educated person.

Minimum Educational Qualification:

To join BSNL in the profile of JTO, a person must be a technical graduate with a Bachelor of Engineering or Bachelor of Technology Degree in specific branch of engineering.

Pay scale: Rs.16400 – Rs.40500/- per month

Junior Telecom Officer (JTO) Profile:

  • Junior Telecom Officer (JTO) is the post for B.Tech in electronics background graduates.
  • JTO is responsible for handling core technical aspects, such as network management, troubleshooting, and operation of network under a specific circle of operation.
  • The role of a JTO at the base-level is considered quite vital in smooth running and maintenance of the entire network at any specific node.
SSC

SSC conducts Entry level examination to recruit engineers from different streams for recruiting Junior Engineers. Junior engineer Examination will be conducted every year in August or September.

Eligibility: Candidate should be completed his degree in Electronic engineering from a recognized university.

Age limit: Minimum age should be 18 years and maximum age for applying is 32 years.

SSC JE Pay Scale:Pay scale will be Rs. 9,300/- to 34,800/- + Grade Pay (GP) Rs. 4,200/-

How to apply?

Eligible candidates can apply through official website. Both online and off line mode of applications are available.

How to prepare?

 The Examination will be conducted in two stages:

  • Written Test (500 marks)
  • Interview (100 marks)

Written Test:

Papers Subjects Maximum Marks Duration
Paper-I Objective type (OMR Answer Sheets will be provided for this Paper) General Intelligence & Reasoning 50 2 Hours
General Awareness 50
General Engineering (Electronic) 100
Paper II  Conventional Type General Engineering (Electronic) 300 2 Hours

 Syllabus:

Questions will be asked from engineering subjects according to your stream.

Genco & Transco

GENCO conducts entry-level exam for B.Tech graduates. GENCO recruits freshers for the post of Assistant engineer.

Written Exam:

  • Section A:

    Questions will be asked on core academic curriculum of respective discipline

  • Section B: Analytical aptitude questions

Assistant Engineer Qualifying Marks: Minimum qualifying marks in this exam for OC category-40%, BC-35%, SC/ST/PH category-30%.

Assistant Engineer Electronic Branch Syllabus:

Basics of circuits and Measurement systems, Transducers, Analog electronics, Digital electronics, Signals and systems, Control systems, Optical communication

  • Assistant engineer supervises, and/or coordinates the work activities of subordinates. Routine engineering problems can be solved by Assistant engineer.
  • Assistant engineer will determine engineering requirements by conducting inspections and analytical tests; analyzing and synthesizing data; maintaining control charts; determining root causes; preparing bid specifications.

Age:

Minimum age to apply 18 years & Maximum age is 44 years.

Selection Process:

Performance in the written examination will be measured along with the personal interview.

Pay Scale: 41,155 – 63,600/-per month

Research Sector

If you want to get in to research, DRDO, ISRO, BARC, etc are what you should aim for. These institutes conduct their own examinations maybe once in 2 or 3 years, depending on their requirements. RRB

RRB

Every year Railway sector conducts their own entrance exam to fill the vacancies in different modules. Railway recruitment board recruits Electronic engineers for the post of senior section engineer.

Senior section Engineer (Electronic):

Age limit: The minimum age to apply for RRB exam is 20 years and maximum age is 34 Years

Pay scale:RS 9300-34800 + GP 4600/- per month

Qualification:Candidate should have completed his Electronic engineering from a recognised university.

Selection Process: Written Examination, Document Verification and Medical test.

  • Selection is made strictly as per merit, on the basis of written examination.
  • The Question papers shall be of Objective Multiple Choice Type. The questions paper will be in English, Hindi, Urdu and local languages.
  • Questions for the written exam will be generally from the educational standards. Objective type multiple choice questions base on General Awareness, Arithmetic, General Intelligence and reasoning, General Science and Technical Ability.
  • For each wrong answer 1/3 marks allotted for each question shall be deducted. The question paper will consist of 200 questions.

Syllabus for Electronic engineer in RRB:

Control Systems, Communication basics, basic electronics, antennas, arrays and all basics of electronic field.

Career path:

  • Assistant Electronic Engineer(Gaz.)
  • Divisional Electronic Engineer
  • Senior Divisional Electronic Engineer

Job description:

  • Senior Section Engineer in Electronics is a supervisor in ECE department of Railways.
  • He can be posted either station or Production Units.
  • If posted in a station he will be in charge of the ECE installations of the station like bulbs, fan, pumps, generators etc.
  • He will have technicians under him to actually do the job.
  • Normally he will have a few small stations of a section under him, which means he has to travel to these stations.

How to Apply: Eligible Interested Candidates are required to apply Online through any one of Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) Website.

Physical standards:

  • Physically fit in all aspects.
  • Visual Standards —Distance Vision: 6/6, 6/6 without glasses.

  • Near Vision: Sn: 0.6,0.6 without glasses (must clear fogging test) and must pass test for Colour Vision, Binocular Vision, Field of Vision & Night Vision.

  • Promotional benefits
