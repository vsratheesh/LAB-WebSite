Why MBA?

State Level MBA Entrance Exams

ICET is the state level entrance exam where you can go for universities like JNTU, OU and affiliated colleges.

National level MBA entrance Exams

GMAT

GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) is also the first choice for top MBA programs like ISB, IIM PGPX, MDI, XLRI and many other that need students to have work experience. GMAT is accepted by 90 B Schools in India.

CAT

To enter in to various management courses in IIMs, IITs and IISC for MBA programs CAT (Common Admission Test) is the Test conducted by IIMs as a qualification. Eligibility to write CAT is minimum 50% aggregate in Graduation. Mostly CAT exam will be conducted every year in the month of November. The safe score is 99.7 percentile for general it varies for others.

MAT

The AIMA (All India Management Association) uses MAT test, for granting admissions to MBA and its equivalent courses. MAT exam will be conducted every year September, December, February and May. You should get 95% marks in the MAT exam to get in to get in to reputed universities.

SNAP

Symbiosis National Aptitude Test (SNAP) is a common written test for the admission to all the PG Institutes of Symbiosis International University which runs management programmes. Eligibility for SNAP is 50% in graduation. Generally this exam will be conducted in December.
