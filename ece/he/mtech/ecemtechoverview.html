M.tech Overview

Are you planning to do M.Tech in Electronics and communication Engineering? Confused about choosing the field of study in higher education?

If you want to improve your technical skills even after B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering, then going for M.Tech is the best option. The tough part here is choosing a specialisation in M.Tech because, you can find different streams which focus on the advanced technologies available in the field of E&C Engineering. Each of the courses will help you to improve your knowledge and skills in the particular field you have chosen. Here we are going to discuss about the How to enter, How to prepare, Different specializations available in M.Tech for E&C Engineers.

Why M.tech
  • M.Tech offers excellent opportunities to take the technological experience to the next stage in that way preparing the candidates for a pretty strong technical career.
  • M.Tech graduates are in high demand not just nationally but internationally too. It is a reality that technically capable people are essential in all the areas and many organizations, including the government will pay well for the right capable persons.
  • M.Tech candidates will also have a high-quality career expansion with supremacy in technical qualifications and knowledge.
How to getin

There are two entrance exams GATE and PGECET to get an admission into M.Tech.

Through GATE you can get admission in IITs, NITs etc. If you want to get placed in one of the top universities, then prepare to the hardest as the GATE score is most important.

PGECET is a state owned examination conducted in both Telangana and Andhra Pradesh. You can join in universities like JNTU, OU and their affiliated colleges.

Remember that the first priority always goes to a GATE qualified student rather than a PGECET qualified student.

PSUs like BHEL, ECIL and IOCL etc. will directly hire the candidates who qualify GATE with a good score.

Remember candidates will be short listed on the basis of their GATE score. Candidates having an IIT B.Tech degree and having a CGPA/CPI score of 8 on 10 are exempted from requirement of GATE qualification.

Let us get into the detailed information on how to get through these two entrance exams

PGECET

PGECET is a State level exam conducted by the top university in the state. Post Graduate Engineering Common Entrance Test (PGECET) locally conducted by the state government which is a common entrance test for admission into regular PG courses in Engineering, Pharmacy or Architecture (M.Tech, M.Pharm and M.Arch). This exam was conducted by the Osmania University in Telangana on behalf of Telangana State Council of Higher Education (TSCHE) and by JNTU Kakinada in Andhra Pradesh on behalf of A.P. State Council of Higher Education (APSCHE).

Eligibility

  • B.Tech graduation with minimum of 55% in any recognized university.
  • Eligible candidates who wish to apply for PGECET may submit their applications through online mode only. It is eligible for all the local colleges within the state.
  • One can get in to good colleges if they can secure rank between from 100-350.
  • There is no negative marking in PGECET.

Registration fee

Registration fee for PGECET will be Rs: 500/- (250/- for SC / ST candidates) for one time. In case a student wants to appear more than once, he has to pay every time he attempt PGECET.

Syllabus for PGECET

The syllabus for PGECET is almost same as GATE syllabus. Go through the official website www.appgecet.org and www.tspgecet.org to get the complete syllabus. Universities like Osmania, JNTU, Kakatiya and JNAFAU offer the PG courses.

Tips while preparing PGECET

Here are a few tips that will allow you to prepare for PGECET.

  • Prepare a study plan for yourself and know the pattern of PGECET
  • Time management is the most important and makes sure every topic is being covered in the syllabus.
  • Always better to revise the previous year’s question papers they indeed help you allot in solving the type of questions that generally come in the examination.
  • Get updated with the current happenings in and around the world. Keep your GK on toes.
  • Being a pro in English will certainly add an extra feather to your hat.
  • There are many online free portals which provide mock tests. Just go for it, it costs nothing but just added proficiency.
GATE

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is an exam conducted all over the India. This Examination tests the comprehensive approach of an engineering student in their respective specialization. Indian institute of science (IISC) and Indian institute of Technology conducts GATE exam once in a year under Ministry of human resource development (MHRD).

Here are few tips that will help you in preparing for GATE:

  • Determination and the confidence that you will clear GATE with excellent scores. This faith is going to play a major role in your success.
  • Have a clear view about GATE, the syllabus and the exam pattern.
  • Concentrate on the syllabus. Understand the subjects involved and then choose the study material that will cover the syllabus completely.
  • Always refer previous question papers, it will help you more.
  • Taking mock tests is important while preparing GATE, so that you can know where you are.
  • Try to solve the papers from Indiabix, Arihant and any other GATE preparatory book.

Subjects to be concentrated by ECE graduate for GATE:

  • Engineering Mathematics (MM, M1,M3)
  • General Aptitude
  • Electronic devices and circuits
  • Control systems
  • Analog and digital communications
  • Electromagnetic waves and transmission lines (EMTL)
  • Network Theory
  • Signals and systems

GATE exam pattern for ECE:

  • Engineering Mathematics (It will have 13% of total marks weigh)
  • General Aptitude (will have 15% of total marks weigh)
  • Subject question (remaining 72% of total marks weigh)

Eligibility:

Any Engineering graduate is eligible to write GATE exam.

Age Limit:

There is no age restriction for the candidates who are willing to apply GATE Exam.

How many times can you attempt?

One can write GATE exam unlimited times, where the score of each attempt is valid for two years.

Time Management:

  • It is better to start with 1 mark Questions (25 Questions). These questions are easy to attempt and will help in building confidence. Then go for 2 marks questions (around 8 questions).
  • Now go for General aptitude section of 10 questions. All these will cover up to 43 questions attempted. All these should be done in a time of 100 to 110 minutes.
  • In technical portion 22 questions are remaining, now you can complete these in 50 minutes comfortably. At the end keep 15 to 20 minutes time for a revision of the answer sheet to ensure all are correct.
  • While attempting the exam, leave the questions which you are not sure because negative marking will be there for wrong answers. So avoid making any guesses and try to eliminate choices by analysis and calculations.

Career Options

After completing M.Tech in Electronics and communication engineering you can good job opportunities within the India as well as Overseas. Manufacturing firms in our country will certainly increase the career options. Some of the areas where you can work after completing M.Tech in E&C Engineering

  • Hardware Manufacturing
  • Home Appliance and VLSI design
  • Power sector
  • Software Engineering/IT
  • Telecommunication

Career options in Research sector

Career Options in abroad
