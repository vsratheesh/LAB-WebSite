How to getin

Any person who wanted to pursue MS, the basic educational qualification must be a bachelor degree. The top most destinations for Masters in Mechanical are Germany, UK, USA, Australia, Canada and Sweden. Indians prefer English-speaking countries most for pursuing MS and of which USA is always their primary choice. Admission requirements change with university and country of choice.

  • For admissions in Germany applicants must have above 3.0 GPA with at least 180 ECTS (European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System) and a minimum IELTS/TOEFL score as fixed by the university concerned.
  • For admissions in US universities, a bachelor’s degree in Mechanical, Electrical and Computer science engineering with a median GPA 3.0 and above with minimum scores in GRE, IELTS/TOEFL is mandatory.
  • For admissions in UK, an aggregate of 70% or above in engineering and a 6.5 IELTS score is compulsory.
  • For admissions in Australia a median of 65% and above in academics and scoring 6.5 in IELTS or 79 in TOEFL is obligatory.

About the course

Masters in Electrical Engineering is such a program where a student research, develops, designs and examine computer and electrical systems, learning all through the classes and lab experiences. Some programs let students to focus in topics such as robotics, communications or network systems. As a way to provide accommodation for students who work, some schools offer part time programs like evening and online classes.

The MS degree in this field usually requires 30-45 semester hours of coursework; some programs suppose students to choose a specialization and to modify classes and research therefore. Students must frequently write and preserve a thesis prior to graduation. Particular topics might include convex optimization, circuit design, microelectronics and nanotechnology; other courses in the program might include, Automatic control, Networking, Image and signal processing, Computer engineering, Energy sources and systems, Fields and optics.

Prerequisites

The Master’s Degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering has three tracks to compete a student’s diverse career and professional goals. Students admitted to the M.S. Program must decide one track to complete for graduation.

  • Course work option: The coursework option does not hold research or thesis needs and instead fills the credit requirement with additional coursework. This track is ideal for students pursuing a Master’s degree for the benefit of registering in additional upper-level coursework.

  • Project Option: The project choice fulfils some credits for the degree requirement all the way through a research project of the student’s own design. The project must be accepted by faculty advisors, must be submitted in an official report, and must be presented openly to audience of peers and instructors. This track is perfect for students who want to perform an in detail research project without writing and shielding an entire thesis.

  • Thesis option: The thesis option is the most conventional Master’s degree track in which a student will execute research, develops and write a thesis, and protect their ideas in a paper. This track is idyllic for students wishing to continue for a Ph.D. degree.

University of Utah admits with eligibility conditions like GPA minimum 3.2 and above, 158 in quant’s 29 in analytical, 150 in verbal scores in GRE, 100 and above in TOFEL, 7.5 and above in IELTS. Applications deadlines are January 15th for fall semester and October 1st Spring Semester. Tuition fee is $16,454* per year.

Career Prospects and Placement opportunities

Agreeing to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), employment for electrical engineers was expected to rise by just 5% over the 2012-2022 decade. This usual growth may be credited to U.S. companies hiring a rising number of competent engineers from other countries at lesser wages. Electrical engineers may find work with state and central governments, manufacturing plants, and scientific and technical organizations. Some electrical engineers cherry-pick to be independent and work as advisors.
