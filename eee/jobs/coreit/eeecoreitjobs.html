Overview

Core - IT

An Electrical engineer is not restricted to automobile/power or allied sectors. As said earlier, there are many fields where an Electrical engineer’s knowledge is applicable. In design, production, manufacturing, supply-chain management and maintenance of machines etc. To understand and implement such projects, one is expected to have knowledge in both Electrical engineering and IT.

Many people after pursuing their B.Tech are going either for higher studies or for IT jobs but core jobs are neglected because lack of information. When we asked people about core jobs their answers were like:

  • Very few options available
  • We are trying but no response from the companies even after cracking the interview.
  • No large openings for EEE in core companies
  • They will not offer high salaries like IT
  • Huge competition of a single post

Here we give you the different core jobs that are available, how to get through them, eligibility, career growth, pay pack, etc,.

Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification:B.Tech in Electrical Engineering

Age Limit:No specific limit mentioned but generally between 21 and 25 years

Work Experience:Generally freshers are taken for the roles like Assistant engineer, system design engineer etc. For roles such as ERP consultant (Eg: Oracle Apps), prior work experience of at least 2 years is required in Electrical domain.

Physical Standards:These careers are less physically demanding than core jobs in automobile or thermal sectors in which you may be expected to exercise physically.

How to enter?

Entrance Exam:Quantitative aptitude, reasoning, general knowledge, domain knowledge (in Electrical engineering) are tested in recruitment test.

Apply Directly: Generally, one needs to pass in the recruitment test even when one applies directly to the company.

Through A Reference: In small companies, you can get a job through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through recruitment test.

How to prepare?

Written Exam: Aptitude and Reasoning refer R S Agarwal Electrical/IT subjects – AutoCAD, MATLAB as they are used for designing

Personal Interview: Revise your core subjects thoroughly and gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

You need to be good with at least a few software tools (such as AutoCAD, Kiel etc.) in addition to knowing your core subjects. You are also expected to know the basics of computers thoroughly.

Job Description & Designation

You should work on tools for designing, production, operations, etc. Designation at entry level includes Systems Engineer, Assistant Systems Engineer, Graduate Engineer, Associate Engineer, etc

Salary and Perks

Salary ranges from 1.5 lakh to 5 lakh depending on the company. One of the most important perks of working in IT-Electrical jobs is that you get to stay in cities. Some companies even provide transport, house, etc.

The Don'ts

Don’t Neglect your core branch. In case you wish to switch career in future, it might come to your rescue.

Don’t get stuck with one company. Remember that in IT, you’ll have more opportunities than you do in Electrical. Salary also increases as you move from one company to another.

Career Switch

You have the option of the going into core, IES, PSU/PSEs, other careers such as Civil Services, Armed Forces, etc.

Remember that scope in IT-Electrical careers is less in government sectors when compared to private sector.

Roles of Design Engg

Roles of Electrical design engineers:

  • Design, implement, maintain, and improve electrical instruments, equipment, facilities, components, products, and systems for commercial, industrial, and domestic purposes.
  • Operate computer-assisted engineering and design software and equipment to perform engineering tasks.
  • Coordinate with manufacturing, construction, installation, maintenance, support, documentation, and testing activities to ensure compliance with specifications, codes, and customer requirements.
  • Inspect completed installations and observe operations, to ensure conformance to design and equipment specifications and compliance with operational and safety standards.

  • Conduct field surveys and study maps, graphs, diagrams, and other data to identify and correct power system problems.
