Overview

PSU Overview

The only jobs that provide a constant source of income are government jobs. PSU’s allow you to get into core sector. If you are into PSUs you get a chance to improve your skills that were learnt in engineering. The possible reasons why people are interested in getting into PSUs are

  • Attractive basic salary which is around 8Lakhs/annum
  • Minimal work pressure
  • Job security

Though many B.Tech graduates want to get into PSU’s, they really do not have any idea about this sector. Further in this article you will read types of PSU’s, the recruitment procedures, eligibility, career growth etc,.

PSUs are divided into 3 categories based on their yearly turn-over of the organization. They are

  • Maharatna (7)
  • Miniratna (17)
  • Navaratana (56)
Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in Computer Engineering

Age Limit: Varies from company to company but generally lies between 21 and 30 years.

Work Experience: Not required for many posts but adds weight during interview and also helps during posting.

Physical Standards: Not general physical standards are mentioned. May vary from role to role. Standards maybe high for floor jobs such as Network/Desktop Engineer.

How to Enter

Entrance Exam: General English, General Awareness, Current Affairs and Computer Science Engineering.

Apply Directly:Students can apply directly to companies which specify the mode of application and entrance exam in the notification.

Through A Reference: Depends on company. Some companies accept applications through reference.

How to Prepare

Written Exam: General Studies/GK – Manorama Year Book, General English – R S Sharma. Computer Engineering –Engineering books.

Personal Interview: Only some PSU/PSEs have personal interviews. These interviews are generally purely technical in nature. You should also read about company profile.

Skill Set

These jobs are primarily technical in nature. However, those who are selected as management trainees will become managers in future. Those selected as MT will be trained by the company as they progress in their career.

Job Description and Designation

Graduate Engineer, Management Trainee, Engineer Trainee and Executive Trainee are the entry-level designation. Fresh recruits are expected to perform technical tasks such as programming, database administration, web designing, network/server/desktop administration etc. of machines.

Salary and Perks

Salary and perks vary from company to company. Minimum salary is Rs. 25,000 and may go up to Rs. 70,000/- in Maharatna and Navratna companies. Some top PSUs such as Indian Oil Corporation (IOC) have unparalleled perks.

Career Progression

Engineer, Senior Engineer, Assistant Managers, Managers...

The Don’ts

  • Lose patience. In PSUs, career growth may not be as fast as in private companies.
  • Despair with posting in remote areas. Though in initial years, you’ll be posted in remote areas, as you grow in career, you’ll be posted in metro areas.

Career Switch

You can enter IES, private sector companies or with the experience of working in public sector, you can choose other careers such as Civil Services, etc.

Why PSU?

The only jobs that provide a constant source of income are government jobs. The reason why PSUs are important is by getting job in PSUs you can get a chance to work with some of the biggest firms in India. If you are in to PSUs you can get a chance of improving your skills you have learnt in your engineering. The possible reasons why people are interested in getting job in PSUs are

  • By joining in PSU you can get a good salary in core sector it is around 8 lakhs per annum where as very few private core companies go for such package.
  • Work pressure will be less when compared to private sector jobs.
  • Job security which is more important for a middle class family.
How to Prepare

Most of the PSU companies recruiting students on the basis of GATE score. So you need to prepare well for GATE exam as the competition is very tough you need to put the best efforts to get the job in PSUs. Apart from core subjects you need to learn General awareness, English and Aptitude, in Computer Engineering most important topics are operating system, databse, automata theory and compiler design etc,.

If your aim is to get in to PSUs make sure that you get a rank below 200 in GATE. To get good rank in you have to answer maximum questions and score more. While preparing for GATE doesn’t skip any subjects. Try to solve previous year GATE papers it will help you a lot, you will get clear idea about important topics and difficulty level by solving previous papers. Always revise the studied topics.

Selection Process

Though interviews are generally held in the fourth year of B.Tech or immediately after you graduate, it is advised that student should start preparing for interviews right from the third year. When you are in college or just graduated, expectations of companies from you are usually low. It is just expected that you know the basics well. On the other hand, if you are attending an interview after 6 months or more after graduation, the interviewer will expect that you have done a little more than what you have studied in college.

What to study for interviews?

As discussed above, you need to be strong with the basics of at least one programming language, database and UNIX commands. Preparation for interviews can be classified as follows

  • Technical – Which is already discussed in detail above
  • Arithmetic and Reasoning – R S Agarwal
  • Soft Skills – This includes oral communication, body language, official letter/email drafting, etc.

You need to focus on improving all the three skills. As it is a time-taking process, you should start early (i.e. at least by third year second semester), especially if you are poor in one or more of the above mentioned skills.

Where to take recruitment training?

Unfortunately,there is not even a single institute in Hyderabad which acts as a finishing school for those graduating in engineering. We have thousands of engineers with good grades but poor technical and soft skills.

     So, you need to gather skills from your college, friends and others.

Pros&Cons

Pros

  • There is no extra working hours and stable source of income.
  • Promotions based on your work.
  • Pension facility is available when you retire.
  • Free medical facility for you and your family members with minimum accommodation charges.
  • Being a Govt employee you will have reorganization/status in society.

Cons

  • Salary growth will be slow when compared with IT sector.
  • If you have any ambition in your life to do something new you cannot achieve that as you are in to Govt job.
  • You have to do respective work every day, jobs will not be challenging to you.
  • You might have to deal with corruption in some form or the other.
