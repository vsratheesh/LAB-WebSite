MS Overview

Are you interested to continue your education in abroad inorder to improve Technical skills? Confused about choosing a course?

If you want to gain more programming skills with practical knowledge about computer science engineering then go for MS. Choosing the specialisation in Masters is the tough job for students. We are giving the specialisations information for students’ inorder to help them. This document will help you in choosing your specialisation; scores to get admit in particular field and career options after MS etc.

Why MS
  • Attracting most of the international student to study in abroad is a big drive.
  • High salaries and lavish life.
  • University excellence, liberal funding, wide selection of courses, flexible core curriculum and strong Indian Communities flourishing abroad.
  • MS gives you an added advantage to your education and will absolutely do well in the long run.
  • When all other competitors have similar technical skills, you will be given first choice over others because qualification does matters.
How to getin

Any person who wanted to pursue MS, the basic educational qualification must be a bachelor degree. Indians prefer English-speaking countries most for pursuing MS and of which USA is always their primary choice. Admission requirements change with university and country of choice.

  • For admissions in Germany applicants must have above 3.0 GPA with at least 180 ECTS (European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System) and a minimum IELTS/TOEFL score as fixed by the university concerned.
  • For admissions in US universities, a bachelor’s degree in Computer science engineering with a median GPA 3.0 and above with minimum scores in GRE, IELTS/TOEFL is mandatory.
  • For admissions in UK, an aggregate of 70% or above in engineering and a 6.5 IELTS score is compulsory.
  • For admissions in Australia a median of 65% and above in academics and scoring 6.5 in IELTS or 79 in TOEFL is obligatory.
Cost to do

Isn't this the first aspect that comes to your mind when you think about studying MS abroad? While a degree abroad isn't shabby for middle class students from India, no doubt there are some international degrees that are less expensive than the others. Indeed, you must add up a list of costs when you decide to study abroad. It can burn a hole in your pocket. If you plan well, you can restrain the damages to the pocket and not end up smouldering the entire shirt. Remember that education is an investment and students have done brilliantly well, not only recovering costs but have even made fortunes.

The cost of studying abroad can be divided into the following

  • English Proficiency Tests-TOEFL/IELTS/PTE
  • Graduate Level Admission Tests-GRE
  • Application fees for the universities you apply
  • Forwarding of test scores to universities (if any)
  • WES Evaluation
  • Courier Expenses
  • Visa and SEVIS fees
  • MS Tuition Costs in USA
  • Student Airfare Costs
  • Cost of living

All in all, with all the individual expenses that the average cost of a MS for international students would be around $36,000 (minimum) depending on the choices you make. Besides the huge numbers you see here, it is not strange to see well planned students who get into great MS colleges with full-grants and graduate with negligible (if not zero) debt.

Master's in Software Engineering

The MS in Software Engineering program provides students with skills that are extensively relevant, extremely demanded and opulently rewarded. Software is an obedience concerned with the processes, methodologies, techniques and tools of mounting high-quality software systems in a money-making manner. The Software Engineering program emphasizes the most excellent software engineering practices, existing methodologies, budding technologies, and their applications in a variety of industries. It also emphasizes the expansion of communication and management skills in a team-based software development atmosphere.

Universities offering Master's in Software Engineering

  • The Pennsylvania State University, USA

Masters in Big Data/Data analytics

Data science and big data deals with garnering great amounts of data and extracting appropriate knowledge and information from it. Usually used in explore activities. Data science is a mix of statistics, computer science and attainments of data breakdown and revelation.

The course covers Big Data technology, advanced analytics and industrial and scientific applications. The Subjects may be Mathematics for Big Data, Python scripting, Big Data theory and computing foundations, Big databases and NoSQL, Analytics, machine learning and data visualization, Optimization and heuristics for big problems, Hadoop and Map Reduce, Scientific and commercial applications.

Universities offering Master's in Big Data/Data Analytics

  • Simon Fraser University, Canada
  • University of Stirling, UK
  • University of San Fransisco, USA
  • Queen Mary University of London, UK
  • Southern New Hampshire University, UK

Prerequisites

For admissions in Simon Fraser University, the qualifications listed are normally recognized as equivalent to a Simon Fraser University 4-year bachelor’s degree with a minimum overall grade of B.

Students must hold a bachelor's degree or equivalent in Computing Science or a related field with a cumulative grade point average (CGPA) of at least 3.00/4.33 (B), or a grade point average (GPA) of at least 3.33/4.33 (B+) based on the last 60 credits of undergraduate courses.

It is expected that successful applicants have programming experience, particularly in languages like Java, Python, and C++.

For English Language Proficiency either 7.0 on IELTS or 93 on TOEFL are required.

Master's in Computer Networks

The Master of Networking (MNet) prepares working networking professionals and graduates with management skills in design, management and maintenance of computer networks. The course develops practical skills in state of the art networks, and in the evaluation, planning and valuation of technologies and network services. MNet emphasizes employability skills in communication networks, partnership and project management. It is a studio-based, project-based and an applied practical course.

Universities offering Master's in Computer Networks

  • Melbourne Institute of Technology, Australia
  • Charles Sturt University, Australia
  • University of Greenwich, UK
  • Boston University, USA
  • Ryerson University, Canada

Prerequisites

For admissions in University of Greenwich, the applicants should have a good degree (2.2 or above) in computer science, computer engineering or a related discipline, Students educated in a language other than English should have an IELTS score of 6.0 or above with no skill less than 5.5.

Career Prospects

Graduates of the Master's in Networking may find employment in leadership roles in network management system administration and ICT services delivery and management within a wide range of public and private enterprises. Typical roles (some with additional work experience) include Network Architect, Network Manager, System Administrator, Senior Sales Engineer, Senior Network Engineer, Senior Systems Engineer, Support Manager, Internet/Intranet Network Manager, Senior Network Analyst, Senior Network Design Engineer, Senior Network Capacity Planner, Senior Network Solutions Architect, Senior Network Security Specialist, Senior Mobile Applications Developer, and Senior System Administrator.

The Bureau of Labor Statistics also indicated that network and computer systems administrators made a median annual wage of $98,430 in 2014.
