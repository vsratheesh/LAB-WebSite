MBA Overview

Are you planning to do MBA after B.Tech? Many candidates are in a dilemma that is it good to do MBA after B.Tech, If you are also one of them, then the below information will definitely give you a clarity about doing MBA after B.Tech.

If you are interested to move into any leadership roles or already you have started a leadership role and need help in doing that role, then MBA is recommended for you. Having work experience before pursuing MBA will be an advantage for you.

MBA is the best option for you If you are not interested to get in to any programming role after B.Tech in EIE. Try to crack CAT if you are planning for 2 years management course from a well established college. But, it is not that easy to crack CAT, you should get 90%le in order to get into IIMS.

Why MBA

The Master of Business Administration (MBA) is a globally well recognized degree intended to expand the skills necessary for careers in business and management. The value of the MBA, though, is not restricted strictly to the trade world.MBA after B.Tech will give an added advantage for your summary, but it is advantageous when you have 2 to 3 years of work experience handier rather than a fresher.

MBA is the most chosen graduate degree in the United States and all over the world. The degree is resourceful and can put forward a major boost up to anyone’s career. Even with its conventional attractiveness, most of them still aren’t convinced what is mixed up in getting an MBA.

Even though, M Tech is the most probable career option after B Tech, a larger percentage of engineering students choose an MBA. Choosing MBA over other PG courses commonly seen as a herd outlook and many educationalists accept as a truth that it’s a deviation from the engineering path.

How to getin

State Level MBA Entrance Exams

ICET is the state level entrance exam where you can go for universities like JNTU, OU and affiliated colleges.

National level MBA entrance Exams

GMAT

GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) is also the first choice for top MBA programs like ISB, IIM PGPX, MDI, XLRI and many other that need students to have work experience. GMAT is accepted by 90 B Schools in India.

CAT

To enter in to various management courses in IIMs, IITs and IISC for MBA programs CAT (Common Admission Test) is the Test conducted by IIMs as a qualification. Eligibility to write CAT is minimum 50% aggregate in Graduation. Mostly CAT exam will be conducted every year in the month of November. The safe score is 99.7 percentile for general it varies for others.

MAT

The AIMA (All India Management Association) uses MAT test, for granting admissions to MBA and its equivalent courses. MAT exam will be conducted every year September, December, February and May. You should get 95% marks in the MAT exam to get in to get in to reputed universities.

SNAP

Symbiosis National Aptitude Test (SNAP) is a common written test for the admission to all the PG Institutes of Symbiosis International University which runs management programmes. Eligibility for SNAP is 50% in graduation. Generally this exam will be conducted in December.

Generally state level engineering colleges offers the following specializations for Graduates.

  • MBA in HR management
  • MBA in Finance
  • MBA in Marketing
  • MBA in Operations Management

To enter in to top highest business schools you have to clear two stages, written test and Personnel interview. You have to attend a Personal Interview once you clear the written test. Here are some of the questions that will ask in personal interview:

  • Tell me about yourself?
  • Why MBA?
  • Why particular Specialisation in MBA?
  • After MBA what is your idea for career?
  • How do you think MBA will be the value addition to your particular degree?
Cost to do

An MBA abroad may cost anywhere from Rs. 20 to 50 lakh, whereas an Indian MBA will cost somewhere between Rs. 7.5 to 25 lakh. MBA Fee Structure will be depends on the Institutes where you are pursuing MBA Course. If it is a Top ranked Business Schools then you have to pay 12 Lakhs to 25 Lakhs. If It is a Tier 2 Business Schools then Fee Structure will be 7 Lakhs to 12 Lakhs. In Normal business Schools fee structure will be up to 4 Lakhs.

Within universities it charges 25000/- to 35000/-

Specilizations

MBA Specializations after B.Tech in ECE

MBA is a two year program, where in the first year the students learn about General Management, Marketing, Accounting, Finance, Organizational Behavior and Business Communication, irrespective of their specialization. While in second year they have to choose a specialization. The most common MBA specializations that are available for any graduate are MBA-HR, MBA-Finance, MBA-Marketing, MBA-Operations and MBA-Systems. You can also have a dual specialization with one Major and with one Minor based on the college that you prefer to study.

How to choose a specialization?

There are few important things that you have to consider before choosing an MBA specialization. But most of the students don’t bother with what they have to specialize. Lets us take a few instances like how a student takes up an MBA specialization.

  • Most girls end up with MBA-HR or MBA-Finance just because they are girls.
  • Most boys end up with MBA-Finance or MBA-Marketing because they are boys.
  • Some end up taking what their friend chooses to study in MBA.
  • Some blindly follow their elders or faculties or seniors
  • Choosing MBA specialization based on previous placement records.

But wait, take the reality check.A student has to check their personality/skill set with the specialization that they want to choose. In a simple way he/she has to have the required skills for the particular specialization. Let us make it simple to understand.

  • If you want to choose MBA-HR then you must have the following skills
    • Commendable communications (verbal & oral)
    • Public Speaking
    • Dealing patience with people
    • Convincing skills
    • Approaching
  • If you want to choose MBA-Marketing then you must have the following skills
    • Should not hesitate to ask
    • Willing to travel
    • Should be open for field work
    • Extrovert
  • If you want to choose MBA-Finance then you must have the following skills
    • Preferable to have B.com/ICWA/CA Inter
    • Excellent Mathematical Skills.
