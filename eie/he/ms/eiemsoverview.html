MS Overview

Are you interested to continue your education in abroad inorder to improve Technical skills? Confused about choosing a course in MS?

If you want to gain more technical skills with practical knowledge about Electronics and instrumentation engineering then go for MS. Choosing the specialisation in Masters is the tough job for students. We are giving the specialisations information for students’ inorder to help them. This document will help you in choosing your specialisation; scores to get admit in particular field and career options after MS in instrumentation engineering etc.

Why MS
  • Attracting most of the international student to study in abroad is a big drive.
  • High salaries and lavish life.
  • University excellence, liberal funding, wide selection of courses, flexible core curriculum and strong Indian Communities flourishing abroad.
  • MS gives you an added advantage to your education and will absolutely do well in the long run.
  • When all other competitors have similar technical skills, you will be given first choice over others because qualification does matters.
How to getin

Any person who wanted to pursue MS, the basic educational qualification must be a bachelor degree. Indians prefer English-speaking countries most for pursuing MS and of which USA is always their primary choice. Admission requirements change with university and country of choice.

  • For admissions in Germany applicants must have above 3.0 GPA with at least 180 ECTS (European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System) and a minimum IELTS/TOEFL score as fixed by the university concerned.
  • For admissions in US universities, a bachelor’s degree in Mechanical, Electrical and Computer science engineering with a median GPA 3.0 and above with minimum scores in GRE, IELTS/TOEFL is mandatory.
  • For admissions in UK, an aggregate of 70% or above in engineering and a 6.5 IELTS score is compulsory.
  • For admissions in Australia a median of 65% and above in academics and scoring 6.5 in IELTS or 79 in TOEFL is obligatory.
Cost to do

Isn't this the first aspect that comes to your mind when you think about studying MS abroad? While a degree abroad isn't shabby for middle class students from India, no doubt there are some international degrees that are less expensive than the others. Indeed, you must add up a list of costs when you decide to study abroad. It can burn a hole in your pocket. If you plan well, you can restrain the damages to the pocket and not end up smouldering the entire shirt. Remember that education is an investment and students have done brilliantly well, not only recovering costs but have even made fortunes.

The cost of studying abroad can be divided into the following

  • English Proficiency Tests-TOEFL/IELTS/PTE
  • Graduate Level Admission Tests-GRE
  • Application fees for the universities you apply
  • Forwarding of test scores to universities (if any)
  • WES Evaluation
  • Courier Expenses
  • Visa and SEVIS fees
  • MS Tuition Costs in USA
  • Student Airfare Costs
  • Cost of living

All in all, with all the individual expenses that the average cost of a MS for international students would be around $36,000 (minimum) depending on the choices you make. Besides the huge numbers you see here, it is not strange to see well planned students who get into great MS colleges with full-grants and graduate with negligible (if not zero) debt.

Career Options

The Electronics and Instrumentation engineering degree opens up a variety of opportunities, especially in the field of Instrumentation, power sector, Production, Designing, Research etc. to name a few.

With the growth in the manufacturing sector, automation industry and Technology the importance of instrumentation Engineering is growing.

Core jobs in instrumentation engineering are available with big public sector undertakings in India like BHEL, GAIL and IOCL, etc., the economic growth in the country has also opened wide opportunities for instrumentation engineers in companies like L&T, Reliance, Siemens, TCS, General Electric, Texas Instruments, National Instruments, Bechtel, Fluor Daniel, etc.
