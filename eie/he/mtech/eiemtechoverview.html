M.tech Overview

Are you planning to do M.Tech in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering? Confused about choosing the field of study in higher education?

If you want to improve your technical skills even after B.Tech in Instrumentation Engineering, then going for M.Tech is the best option. The tough part here is choosing a specialisation in M.Tech because, you can find different streams which focus on the advanced technologies available in the field of Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering. Each of the courses will help you to improve your knowledge and skills in the particular field you have chosen. Here we are going to discuss about the How to enter, How to prepare, Different specializations available in M.Tech for Electronics and Instrumentation Engineers.

Why M.tech
  • M.Tech offers excellent opportunities to take the technological experience to the next stage in that way preparing the candidates for a pretty strong technical career.
  • M.Tech graduates are in high demand not just nationally but internationally too. It is a reality that technically capable people are essential in all the areas and many organizations, including the government will pay well for the right capable persons.
  • M.Tech candidates will also have a high-quality career expansion with supremacy in technical qualifications and knowledge.
How to getin

You can apply for M.Tech in two ways

Through GATE

Through Graduate aptitude test of engineering (GATE) you can get admission in IITs, NITs etc. If your interest is to pursue your Post graduate degree from top universities then it advisable to prepare well for GATE exam.

Through PGECET

PGECET is a state owned examination conducted in both Telangana and Andradesh. You can join in universities like JNTU, OU and their affiliated colleges.

Foremost priority will be given to GATE qualified student than a PGECET qualified student.

PSUs like BHEL, ECIL and IOCL etc. will directly hire the candidates who qualify GATE.

PGECET

PGECET is a State level exam conducted by the top university in the state. Post Graduate Engineering Common Entrance Test (PGECET) locally conducted by the state government which is a common entrance test for admission into regular PG courses in Engineering, Pharmacy or Architecture (M.Tech, M.Pharm and M.Arch). This exam was conducted by the Osmania University in Telangana on behalf of Telangana State Council of Higher Education (TSCHE) and by JNTU Kakinada in Andhra Pradesh on behalf of A.P. State Council of Higher Education (APSCHE).

Eligibility

  • B.Tech graduation with minimum of 55% in any recognized university.
  • Eligible candidates who wish to apply for PGECET may submit their applications through online mode only. It is eligible for all the local colleges within the state.
  • One can get in to good colleges if they can secure rank between from 100-350.
  • There is no negative marking in PGECET.

Registration fee

Registration fee for PGECET will be Rs: 500/- (250/- for SC / ST candidates) for one time. In case a student wants to appear more than once, he has to pay every time he attempt PGECET.

Syllabus for PGECET

The syllabus for PGECET is almost same as GATE syllabus. Go through the official website www.appgecet.org and www.tspgecet.org to get the complete syllabus. Universities like Osmania, JNTU, Kakatiya and JNAFAU offer the PG courses.

Tips while preparing PGECET

Here are a few tips that will allow you to prepare for PGECET.

  • Prepare a study plan for yourself and know the pattern of PGECET
  • Time management is the most important and makes sure every topic is being covered in the syllabus.
  • Always better to revise the previous year’s question papers they indeed help you allot in solving the type of questions that generally come in the examination.
  • Get updated with the current happenings in and around the world. Keep your GK on toes.
  • Being a pro in English will certainly add an extra feather to your hat.
  • There are many online free portals which provide mock tests. Just go for it, it costs nothing but just added proficiency.
GATE

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is an exam conducted all over the India. This Examination tests the comprehensive approach of an engineering student in their respective specialization. Indian institute of science and Indian institute of Technology conducts GATE exam once in a year under Ministry of human resource development (MHRD).

By writing GATE exam you will have various options like you can pursue higher Education (M.Tech, M.E). The other option you will have is apply for a job in various PSUs that hire B.Tech graduates through GATE score. MDL, CEL, BPCL, Indian oil, HPCL, GAIL, BHEL, ONGC, Power grid, ISRO, DRDO etc. are the PSUs hire Electrical graduates based on GATE score.

If your GATE rank is below 1000 then you can get in to best universities like NITs, IITs, and state universities (JNTU, OU). If you are pursuing your PG program through GATE then universities will offer you stipend, minimum stipend you can get is RS.8000/- which varies based on colleges.

How to prepare?

As an Electrical student you should concentrate more on your core subjects throughout 4 years of your B.Tech. The following subjects are important while you are preparing for GATE exam.

  • Engineering Mathematics
  • General Aptitude
  • Basics of Electrical and electronic engineering
  • Network Theory
  • Control system
  • Power system
  • Electrical Machine
  • Power Electronics
  • Signal and systems

It is advisable to go for GATE coaching in the 3rd year 1st semester to get an idea about the exam pattern, Syllabus etc. Then go for GATE coaching one more time in your 4th year so that you can get perfection.

GATE exam pattern for EIE

Engineering Mathematics (It will have 13% of total marks weigh)

General Aptitude (will have 15% of total marks weigh)

Subject question (remaining 72% of total marks weigh)

Eligibility

Any Engineering graduate is eligible to write GATE exam.

Age Limit

There is no age restriction for the candidates who are willing to apply GATE Exam.

How many times can you attempt?

One can write GATE exam unlimited times, where the score of each attempt is valid for two years.

Time Management

  • It is better to start with 1 mark Questions (25 Questions). These questions are easy to attempt and will help in building confidence. Then go for 2 marks questions (around 8 questions).
  • Now go for General aptitude section of 10 questions. All these will cover up to 43 questions attempted. All these should be done in a time of 100 to 110 minutes.
  • In technical portion 22 questions are remaining, now you can complete these in 50 minutes comfortably. At the end keep 15 to 20 minutes time for a revision of the answer sheet to ensure all are correct.
  • While attempting the exam, leave the questions which you are not sure because negative marking will be there for wrong answers. So avoid making any guesses and try to eliminate choices by analysis and calculations.

Advantages of GATE

  • Many public sector undertakings are using GATE score for selecting candidates for their organizations.
  • During M.Tech course the student can get stipend or RS 8, 000/- per month (For the students who went through GATE).
  • A good rank in GATE assures a best job.
  • After joining M.Tech. in IITs and IISc, one can look at a salary package stretching from 7-30 lakhs/annum, subject to specialisation and performance.
  • You can even go for Junior Research Partnership jobs in CSIR laboratories only if you get good score in GATE.
  • The students who qualify this test are eligible for doing Master’s degree at National University of Singapore– NUS.

Tips to score well in GATE exam

  • Refer previous years question papers along with solutions, analyse the exam pattern and focus on those subjects which have maximum marks.
  • Read books which will give you conceptual clarity.
  • Don’t ever just depend on one book for referring the tops. Have different options for the syllabus.
  • Keep in mind that those topics which are not a part of GATE syllabus could be undoubtedly left out.
  • Make a list of topics in which you are weak and focus on those. Learn shortcuts for solving problems.
  • Joining a coaching institute is beneficial as you would be exposed to a regular systematic study.
  • Complete the theory preparation one month before the exam and then practice it number of times.
  • Stay updated about there is any changes or developments in the GATE exam for the coming year.

Career Options

Job roles of an Instrumentation Engineer

You can find various career options after completing M.Tech in Instrumentation Engineering.

  • Design and develop control systems
  • Maintain the existing control systems
  • Manage the control systems
  • Collaborate with design engineers, purchasers and other staff members involved in the production processes
  • Manage projects within the given restraints including cost and time
  • Troubleshoot

Job Opportunities for Instrumentation Engineers

Instrumentation engineers can find employment in industries which improve efficiency, stability and safety of automated processes. The industrial sectors include

  • Public/Private firms
  • R&D organizations
  • Steel plants
  • Cement manufacturing companies
  • Thermal power plants
  • Chemical companies etc.
