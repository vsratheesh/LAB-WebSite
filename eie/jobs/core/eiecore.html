Overview

Core Jobs Overview

Electronics and Instrumentation engineering graduates who have passion for their core subjects often wish to enter careers in the private sector like automobile, thermal power, Instrumentation or other allied sectors.

We will help you to choose a career with their respective eligibility in 10 easy steps. It helps you more in comparing yourself with the eligibility of any career. This document will easily help you to hold a knowledge on how a core sector works and its eligibility criteria.

Ten Things

Eligibility criteria

Academic Qualification: B.Tech in EIE

Age Limit: No specific limit mentioned but generally between 21 and 25 years

Work Experience: Either a fresher or one who is experienced can enter the core field. Companies selecting students through on campus interview take fresher’s while some companies take only experienced candidates.

Physical Standards: Core jobs are more physically demanding since you may be required to stand for long hours and perform physically strenuous tasks while working on machines.

How to Enter

Entrance Exam: Companies primarily test knowledge in EIE stream. Students should be thorough with subjects such as Control systems, Electronic Devices, Analog Circuits, Digital Circuits, Signals and Systems, Control Systems, Communications, Electromagnetism, Mathematics etc

Apply Directly: Students can apply to companies such as GE, L & T, Hyundai etc., directly after completing B.Tech. The percentage requirement for core sector companies and core roles is generally higher than for non-core roles.

Through A Reference: In small companies, you can get in through reference. In some MNCs, special employee referral drives are conducted sometimes while some big companies only accept through entrance test.

How to Prepare

Written Exam: Instrumentation Engineering – Authors will change from subject to subject, Aptitude and Reasoning – R S Agarwal.

Personal Interview: Revise your core subjects thoroughly, especially those mentioned in the above ‘Entrance Exam’ section and try to gain knowledge on the company (such as company profile, clients, product line, etc.)

Skill Set

Core companies expect students to be sound with basic concepts of Instrumentation engineering. Depending on the profile of the company and the role for which the student is being taken, companies may test his/her knowledge on a particular topic.

Job Description and Designation

You should work from processing level based on company’s respective product, or if working for any automobile company one should work in car service stations to gain complete knowledge of the product Designation at entry level includes Junior Engineer, Engineer, Assistant Engineer, Supervisor etc.

Salary and Perks

Top core companies such as Honda, Tata Motors pay decent packages while small companies pay much less. Some core sector roles such as quality engineer have the scope of travelling. In addition to bearing the expenses, companies also provide incentives to engineers.

Salary

In private sector, one can expect more income when compared to the government sector. The compensation will be based on the colleges they have studies for example: IIT candidate can get more compensation than normal college students.

Companies hire students through campus placements and then train them according to their requirements. Campus placement is the best and easiest way to get a job in a top company. As a fresher you can join as an engineer or trainee based on the requirement and the skills you possess.

  • IIT mechanical engineer: 30,000 INR to 60,000 INR per month (Basic Pay)
  • Other mechanical engineer: 10,000 INR to 25,000 INR per month (Basic Pay)
